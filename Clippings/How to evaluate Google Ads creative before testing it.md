## Evaluate Google Ads creative with the MOCA framework before it enters testing, so you're experimenting with stronger ideas from the start.

If you manage Google Ads, you’re responsible for creative that runs across surfaces and formats that never used to exist.

Not long ago, an ad was a single headline and two short descriptions. Today, a single Google ad can include multiple responsive text assets, videos, and images, generating [tens of thousands of permutations](https://searchengineland.com/how-to-assemble-captivating-google-ads-copy-459469).

We’re often told that when it comes to ad creative, we should just “let the data decide.” So mediocre assets enter rotation, the platform reports on performance, and nobody can explain why an ad works or doesn’t.

Part of your job is to champion good ideas and push back on bad ones. Creative direction isn’t about overriding your tests. It’s about improving the quality of the ideas that enter them.

That takes creative judgment, and it’s a skill you can build. At SMX Advanced 2026, I shared the framework I use to evaluate ads like a creative director. It’s called MOCA, which stands for magnetic, obvious, congruent, and actionable.

Let’s walk through each pillar using real ads.

## Magnetic ads qualify the click

Magnets are selectively attractive. They pull some things in and actively push others away.

That’s exactly how great ads work. You want to attract buyers and repel tire kickers because every unqualified click is budget you’ll never get back.

Look at these two ads running on the same “investing” keyword:

![Image 159](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-159.png)

- Get Started with $1
- $500 Min Investment

Robinhood says you can start investing with just $1. That’s clearly for beginners.

Meanwhile, Percent promotes a $500 minimum investment for accredited investors. That’s clearly for high-net-worth individuals.

Both ads qualify the click before the visit ever happens.

Here are two more examples of magnetic ads:

![Image 164](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-164.png)

- Find ISO 27001 Gaps Before Auditors
- Crazy Comfortable 4-Way Stretch

If you’re preparing for an ISO 27001 audit, the first ad grabs your attention. If you’re not, you scroll right past it.

If you like stretchy jeans, Mugsy’s shopping ad earns your click immediately. If you’re not in the market, there’s no reason to click.

Your ad should make it obvious whether someone should click or skip.

In a broad match world, [qualifying the click with creative](https://searchengineland.com/broad-targeting-creative-qualifier-481267) is the difference between funding your growth and funding Google’s.

Magnetic ads qualify the click, so your budget doesn’t have to.

Does it tell viewers whether to click or skip?

- **Attract:** Does your target audience want what’s being offered?
- **Repel:** Would your [anti-audience](https://searchengineland.com/transform-google-ads-headlines-anti-audiences-440004) self-select out before clicking?

## Obvious ads don’t make you think

An obvious ad is self-evident. Someone with zero context — your aunt, let’s say — should immediately understand what’s being offered without decoding anything. You’d be surprised how many ads fail this test.

Non-obvious ads usually fall into two categories: the “mystery ad” and the “information dump.” Here are examples of each:

![Image 163](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-163.png)

- Start for Free. How will you use it?
- Measure what matters: lasting change

The first ad is a mystery ad. The image is cryptic, and the headline asks, “How will you use it?” Use what, exactly? It’s not clear what the offer even is.

The second ad is an information dump. Three scorecards and a wall of tiny text fill the image, yet it still takes effort to understand what the ad is actually about.

These next two ads both promote apps, but one is immediately obvious, and the other is confusing:

![Image 162](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-162.png)

- Earn While You Walk! Walk and earn coins daily
- Discover the joy of cheerful Pikmin planting flowers around you

“Earn while you walk” is an obvious headline for a step-tracking app. “Discover the joy of cheerful Pikmin planting” isn’t, and the image does nothing to clarify it.

### The paradox of ‘magnetic’ vs. ‘obvious’

At first glance, “magnetic” and “obvious” seem to contradict each other. Magnetic ads narrow the audience. Obvious ads should be universally understood.

So how can an ad be both? How do you write something that’s only for your target audience and still instantly clear to everyone?

The answer is simpler than it sounds. I don’t have to want what you offer, but I do have to know what category it belongs to.

Take another look at the ISO 27001 audit service ad. I don’t know what ISO 27001 is. But I know with absolute certainty that I’m not preparing for one. So when I see the ad, I know I can safely skip it. The ad doesn’t need to explain ISO 27001. It just needs to be obvious enough that I immediately recognize, “This isn’t for me.”

That’s the real job of an obvious ad: not to persuade everyone, but to make the sorting happen instantly.

Would your aunt know what it’s about?

- **Defined offer:** Is the offer and its payoff clear? Do people understand what it is and what they’ll get?
- **Reinforcement:** Does every asset (text, image, and video) reinforce the same idea?

## Congruent ads meet the user where they actually are

Most of us think “congruence” means message match: the ad should reflect the [landing page](https://searchengineland.com/high-converting-ppc-landing-page-tips-395393). That matters, but it’s only part of the equation. The more important aspect of congruence is matching the offer to user intent.

Here are two ads for handheld devices:

![Image 161](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-161.png)

- Try it risk-free. Free shipping, free returns
- Accept Payments Anywhere. Sell faster…

The first ad doesn’t connect the product to the prospect’s actual goal. Nobody wakes up hoping to get free returns on a mystery device.

Contrast that with Square’s “Accept Payments Anywhere. Sell faster.” You immediately understand what it does and whether it solves your problem.

These next two ads for schools offer an even starker contrast:

![Image 166](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-166.png)

- HBS Online Business Strategy. Build an understanding of AI
- You are In. Now Deposit. Your college journey begins when you deposit

The HBS ad describes the program. The second jumps straight to payment options in a prospecting ad. It’s the embodiment of the advice, “Don’t ask for marriage on the first date.”

Does it meet your user where they actually are?

- **Mind matching:** Does it match what’s on your user’s mind, not just the keyword?
- **Ad-to-landing-page alignment:** Does the promise match the landing page?

## Actionable ads drive the next step

You want your audience saying, “Here, take my money,” because they’re eager for what’s on the other side of the click. Clicking should feel like a no-brainer.

One of these ads tells you what you’ll get after you click. The other doesn’t.

![Image 167](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-167.png)

- Private 5+ Acreage Near Nashville. Land & Cabin Combo $119,000
- Built Into The Culture Of Schindler. Explore Our Products

The private acreage ad is essentially one big call to action (CTA), telling you exactly what you’ll get after you click.

The second ad doesn’t even imply a call to action. It references company culture, not the product line. Urgency and exclusivity also strengthen CTAs.

![Image 165](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-165.png)

- 50% Off WSJ’s Best Budgeting App of 2025
- We Deliver Daily Dose Text. Text designed for busy people…

“50% off” is a real reason to click today. Meanwhile, “My Daily Dose” is trying to sell text-message subscriptions to busy people who already resent their notifications. There’s no path forward that feels good.

An actionable ad makes it clear what happens after the click and why it’s worth taking.

Does clicking feel like a no-brainer?

- **CTA clarity:** Is it clear what happens after you click?
- **Reason to act now:** Does your ad give people a reason to click now instead of later?

## Score your ads with the MOCA matrix

To make this framework more practical, I vibecoded a free MOCA scoring matrix.

![Image 160](https://searchengineland.com/wp-content/seloads/2026/07/image-160.png)

It won’t score ads for you. That would defeat the purpose of developing your own creative evaluation skills. Instead, you’ll evaluate each ad against the four MOCA pillars: magnetic, obvious, congruent, and actionable.

A high score doesn’t guarantee an ad will win. It means the ad is worth testing. MOCA is quality control before your [creative enters testing](https://searchengineland.com/measure-demand-gen-creative-impact-asset-uplift-tests-474868).

Creative judgment is still yours. MOCA gives you a way to apply it and defend it in front of clients, CMOs, and data purists.
